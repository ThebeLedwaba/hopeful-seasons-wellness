3. NATURE AND PURPOSE OF PSYCHOTHERAPY

Psychotherapy for children may involve:

  • Talking, play-based, art-based, or activity-based therapeutic methods
  • Emotional, behavioural, social, or developmental support
  • Parent guidance sessions when clinically indicated

The purpose of therapy is to support the child’s psychological wellbeing and development. Outcomes cannot be guaranteed.

4. CONFIDENTIALITY

All information shared in therapy is treated as confidential. However, confidentiality cannot be guaranteed in the following circumstances:

  • If there is a risk of harm to the child or others
  • If abuse or neglect is disclosed (mandatory reporting applies)
  • If records are subpoenaed by a court of law

Parents will receive general feedback about progress, themes, and recommendations. Specific disclosures made by the child will remain confidential unless disclosure is clinically or legally required.

5. PARENTAL INVOLVEMENT

Parents may be involved through:

  • Initial assessment and intake
  • Feedback sessions
  • Periodic review meetings

The psychologist determines the level of parental involvement in the child’s best interest.

6. FEES, CANCELLATIONS, AND PAYMENT

  • Fees are payable as per the practice fee structure
  • Cancellations must be made at least 24 hours in advance
  • Late cancellations or non-attendance may be billed in full

Medical aid claims (if applicable) remain the responsibility of the parent/guardian.

7. RECORD KEEPING & DATA PROTECTION

Psychological records are maintained securely in accordance with POPIA and HPCSA guidelines. Records are retained for the legally required period.
